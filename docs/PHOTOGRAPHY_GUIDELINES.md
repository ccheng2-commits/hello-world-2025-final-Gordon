### Recommended Equipment
- **Camera**: Canon 5D Mark IV (or similar DSLR)
- **Lens**: 60mm macro lens

### Camera Settings

#### Exposure Settings
- **ISO**: 100-400 (lower is better for quality)
- **Aperture**: f/5.6 - f/8 (good depth of field, sharp focus)
- **Shutter Speed**: 1/125s or faster (avoid motion blur)
- **Exposure Mode**: Manual or Aperture Priority

#### Focus Settings
- **Focus Mode**: Single-point autofocus (AF-S / One Shot)
- **Focus Point**: Center point on pupil/iris
- **Focus Method**: Manual fine-tuning recommended for macro work

#### White Balance
- **Mode**: Auto or Daylight (5500K)
- **Avoid**: Fluorescent or Tungsten (can cause color shifts)

## üìê Composition & Framing

### Image Size Requirements
- **Minimum Resolution**: 50,000 pixels (e.g., 224x224)
- **Recommended**: 100,000 - 500,000 pixels
- **Maximum**: No strict limit (higher is better)

### Framing Guidelines
- **Subject**: Single eye, centered
- **Crop**: Eye should fill 30-70% of frame
- **Background**: Can be dark or light (doesn't matter for processing)
- **Orientation**: Portrait or landscape both acceptable

### Distance & Angle
- **Distance**: Close enough to see iris texture clearly
- **Angle**: Straight-on view (perpendicular to eye)
- **Avoid**: Extreme angles (side view, looking up/down)

## üéØ Focus & Sharpness

### Critical Focus Points
1. **Pupil**: Must be sharp and clearly defined
2. **Iris Texture**: Radial patterns should be visible
3. **Iris Rings**: Concentric rings should be distinguishable

### Focus Technique
1. Use single-point autofocus on pupil center
2. If autofocus struggles, use manual focus with live view
3. Take multiple shots at slightly different focus distances
4. Check sharpness on camera LCD (zoom in to verify)

### Sharpness Indicators
- ‚úÖ **Good**: Iris texture clearly visible
- ‚úÖ **Good**: Pupil edge is sharp (not blurry)
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Poor**: Overall softness or motion blur

---

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Issues to Avoid

### 1. Overexposure
- **Problem**: Brightness > 200, loss of detail
- **Solution**: Reduce exposure, use smaller aperture or faster shutter

### 2. Underexposure (Extreme)
- **Problem**: Brightness < 20, no detail visible
- **Solution**: Increase light or exposure (but 24.6 is acceptable)

### 3. Motion Blur
- **Problem**: Subject or camera movement
- **Solution**: Faster shutter speed (1/125s+), use tripod, chin rest

### 4. Out of Focus
- **Problem**: Soft focus, no clear iris texture
- **Solution**: Check focus before shooting, use manual focus if needed

### 5. Poor Contrast
- **Problem**: Low contrast (< 40 std dev)
- **Solution**: Better lighting setup, adjust exposure

### 6. Wrong Angle
- **Problem**: Eye at extreme angle
- **Solution**: Ensure straight-on view, use chin rest

## üí° Pro Tips

1. **Take Multiple Shots**: Capture 3-5 images, select best one
2. **Check Focus**: Always review on camera LCD, zoom in to verify
3. **Consistent Setup**: Use same lighting and camera settings for consistency
4. **Chin Rest**: Essential for stable positioning
5. **Macro Lens**: 60mm macro provides ideal working distance
6. **Diffused Light**: Avoid harsh shadows or direct flash
7. **Patient Subject**: Ensure subject is comfortable and still
